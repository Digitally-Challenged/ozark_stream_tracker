# Caddo River

## Overview
**Rating:** Class I-II
**Watershed Size:** L
**Gradient:** Gentle
**Length:** ~82 miles total
**Season:** March-June best; year-round possible

## Description
The Caddo River winds through the Ouachita Mountains from Black Springs to DeGray Lake, offering some of the best family-friendly floating in Arkansas. This pool-and-drop river is gentle enough for novice paddlers yet with enough Class I and II rapids along the way to keep it exciting. Clear water, gravel bars for lunch stops, and views ranging from rocky outcrops to forested hillsides make it a classic Ouachita float. The river is ideal for all levels of floating experience.

## Sections

### Caddo Gap to Glenwood (7.5 mi)
- **Character:** Most popular section
- **Features:** Mix of calm water and Class I-II rapids
- **Notes:** Houses to hillsides to rocky outcrops, gravel bars for rest stops

### Glenwood to Amity (8 mi)
- **Character:** More whitewater, some flat stretches
- **Features:** Class II-III at higher flows
- **Notes:** Check flows - above 250 cfs recommended

### Norman to Amity (29 mi)
- **Character:** Longer wilderness float
- **Features:** Class I-II throughout
- **Notes:** Multi-day trip option

## Access Points

- **Norman** - Upper access
- **Caddo Gap** - Popular put-in for day floats
- **Glenwood** - Mid-river access, multiple outfitters
- **Amity** - Lower take-out

## Rapids & Features
- Pool-and-drop character
- Class I-II rapids throughout
- Gravel bars for camping and lunch
- Rocky outcrops and scenic bluffs

## Hazards
- Strainers in some sections
- Glenwood to Amity requires good line selection or you'll end up in rocks/trees
- Higher water increases difficulty

## Gauge Information
**Primary Gauge:** Caddo R. nr Caddo Gap (07359610)
**Optimal Range:** 5.3-7.25 ft
